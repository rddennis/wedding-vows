What We Do

Matrimonial Vows

We believe your matrimonial vows are the most important part of your wedding day. When the cake is eaten, and the guests have returned home, your vows will be the promises that guide you through your life together, and through the joy and happiness, and inevitable trials and tribulations, of marriage. These words should convey your willingness to support your partner not only through sickness and in health, but also in times of turmoil. Before writing your matrimonial vows, we’d like to learn about your relationship, including your most memorable moments together, and the point at which you knew you’d found your one. From there, let the words flow from our pens, to your mouth, and into your partner’s heart.

Honor Attendant Speeches

Whether a Maid, Matron, or Man of Honor, or the Best Man or Woman, one of your duties is to provide a speech that shares stories of your adventures, growth, and love toward the newly married couple. A wedding day celebrates the union of two parties, and though your relationship may be stronger with one newlywed, your Honor Attendant speech should express your love and well-wishes for the couple. Honor Attendant speeches are one of our specialties, and we do our best to write with humor, love, and the most candid of emotions. If there’s ever a time to tell the couple how much you love them, it’s on their special day.

Parental Speeches

What a joy it is to see the child you’ve raised become an adult who will share the rest of their lives with someone else. As a Parent of Honor, you’re passing the torch of trusted care and responsibility to your child’s partner. During your parental speech, you’re honoring your child for their accomplishments and welcoming a new member to your family unit. Our parental speeches will commemorate the joy of the day, while sharing delightful memories of your loved one’s childhood with a room of their most valued friends and family. Your job as a parent is never complete, and through your parental speech, you’ll let your child know that you’ll continue to love, cherish, and support them, and their new partner, unconditionally.
